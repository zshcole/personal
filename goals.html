looking ahead to 2025 and beyond

my career goals have evolved significantly since i started in tech. initially, i thought working at a big tech company was the ultimate goal, but i've learned that impact and fulfillment matter more than prestige.

short-term, i want to deepen my expertise in modern web technologies and become a go-to person for complex frontend challenges. this means mastering advanced React patterns, exploring emerging frameworks, and understanding how to build truly scalable applications.

medium-term goals focus on technical leadership and mentorship. i want to lead teams that build products people actually love using. this involves not just writing good code, but also helping other developers grow and creating engineering cultures that prioritize both quality and speed.

i'm particularly interested in the intersection of e-commerce and developer experience. platforms like Shopify and Salesforce Commerce Cloud are transforming how businesses operate online, and i want to be part of building the next generation of these tools.

long-term, i see myself in one of two paths:

• becoming a principal engineer at a company building developer tools or platforms
• starting my own company focused on making web development more accessible and enjoyable
• contributing to open source projects that have meaningful impact on the developer community

what drives me is the belief that good software can make people's lives significantly better. whether that's helping a small business owner set up their first online store or building tools that let developers ship features faster, i want my work to have tangible positive impact.

i'm also committed to continuous learning. the tech landscape changes rapidly, and staying current requires constant curiosity and adaptation. i want to maintain that beginner's mindset throughout my career, always looking for better ways to solve problems.

ultimately, success for me means building things that last, working with people i respect, and knowing that my contributions made someone's day a little bit easier.
